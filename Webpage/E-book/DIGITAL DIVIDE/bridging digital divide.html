Bridging The Divide

The idea that some information and communication technologies are vital to a quality civic life is not new. It’s hard to imagine a life without computers, but even for a country as wired as the U.S, there is still a wide gap between those who have access technology and the skills to benefit from it. Some government initiatives have been set up to reduce the gap in the information for example digital villages which enable people who don’t have access to the internet are able to enjoy the luxuries of the internet. For example in Kenya people are bale to open new websites for their small businesses with help from the government. More schools in the rural areas are being provided with computers to reduce the computer litteracy levels in the area. With new technology advancement people are able to purchase these items, which is a method of reducing the technologic divide. Some large scale companies like Microsoft are promoting some initiatives which will reduce computer literacy. All in all most of the gap is being reduced by government initiatives.
